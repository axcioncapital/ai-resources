# Analytical Output Principles

> **When to read this file:** When producing analytical outputs — research synthesis, document production, claim reframing — for the rules on evidence/interpretation separation, source provenance, and uncertainty handling.

These principles govern analytical-work outputs (research synthesis, document production, claim reframing). They do not apply to routine command/skill/edit turns.

- **Separate evidence from interpretation.** When producing analytical outputs, explicitly separate Evidence (sourced claims with attribution) from Interpretation (analytical conclusions). Interpretation must not reference other interpretation as if it were evidence.
- **Provided sources only.** Analytical outputs must draw only from the evidence supplied for the task. Do not incorporate claims from model training data. When the evidence base does not support a needed claim, mark the gap explicitly — `[CITATION NEEDED]`, `[EVIDENCE GAP]`, or equivalent — rather than filling from general knowledge. Applies to every synthesis context in the workspace, not only research-workflow stages.
- **Admit uncertainty over fabrication.** When evidence does not support a confident answer to an analytical or factual question, state the limitation explicitly (e.g., "I don't have enough information to confidently assess this") rather than generating a plausible-sounding answer. Hedging, scope caveats, and proxy framing are acceptable when labeled; implicit smoothing is not.
