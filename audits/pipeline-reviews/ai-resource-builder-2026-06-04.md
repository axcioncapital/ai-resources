# Pipeline Review — ai-resource-builder — 2026-06-04

## Summary

`ai-resource-builder` is the shared methodology engine behind the canonical creation pipeline — `/create-skill`, `/improve-skill`, and `/migrate-skill` all read its body for their Create/Improve workflows and pass its `references/evaluation-framework.md` to their QC subagents. Its design center is correct: a single-responsibility, three-mode skill (Create / Evaluate / Improve) with progressive disclosure across six one-level-deep reference files, sitting at 433 lines under the 500-line budget. It is load-bearing per DR-2 (canonical pipelines) and the registry selection criterion — degrade it and skill quality across the whole library degrades. The shape risk it carries today is contract-coupling drift, not structural rot: the three consuming commands depend on its section names and the evaluation-framework path, and that coupling is currently consistent but undocumented inside the skill. Currency-check against the pinned Anthropic Agent Skills doc passes — frontmatter conventions match current platform guidance. The pipeline is current and sound; the findings below are refinements, not repairs.

## Innovations proposed

- **Record the consumer contract inside SKILL.md** — the three pipeline commands depend on the exact section names "Create Workflow" / "Improve Workflow" and on the `references/evaluation-framework.md` path; renaming either silently breaks all three. This is a two-end contract of the class `risk-topology.md § 5` flags ("Change modifies a string literal matched by another component"). A short "Consumers" note naming `/create-skill`, `/improve-skill`, `/migrate-skill` would make the coupling loud per OP-3, not silent. Affects: `skills/ai-resource-builder/SKILL.md`. Risk class: minor (additive prose, no behavioral change).
- **State that `model:`/`effort:` are Claude-Code-harness fields, not platform-doc fields** — the SKILL teaches both as "required for every skill," but the pinned Anthropic doc lists only `name`/`description` as required. The harness honors `model:`/`effort:`; the platform does not require them. A one-clause gloss prevents a future authoring Claude from "correcting" the skill against upstream docs and stripping the repo convention. Grounds in QS-5 (tier matches task). Affects: `skills/ai-resource-builder/SKILL.md` § Frontmatter, `references/operational-frontmatter.md`. Risk class: minor.
- **Promote the `name` reserved-word / XML-tag rule into the Name Field section** — the platform doc bars "anthropic"/"claude" substrings and XML tags in `name`; the SKILL's Name Field section states only the char/case rule and the folder-match rule. `writing-standards.md` mentions reserved words in passing but the authoritative Name section omits them. Closing this makes the create-time validation catch a real platform reject. Affects: `skills/ai-resource-builder/SKILL.md` § Name Field. Risk class: minor.
