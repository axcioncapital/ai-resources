# Pipeline Review — `/prime` — 2026-05-29

## Summary

`/prime` is the session-entry orchestrator: it pulls the cwd-repo and ai-resources, reads the log-trio (session-notes + decisions + usage-log), cross-checks Next Steps against git log to filter likely-DONE bullets, detects same-day sibling entries, surfaces a resumable scratchpad, scans `next-up.md` and friction/improvement logs for menu candidates, runs four exception checks (working tree, model alignment, pull result, phase READMEs), and emits a ≤3-item plain-English brief that branches into three completion modes (8a numbered task, 8b free-text intent, 8c auto). The design center is loud-failure over silent state: every claim is cross-checked against a second source before being surfaced, and every brief line is suppressed unless abnormal. Shape risk is moderate and concentrated in two-end string contracts — `/prime` 8c.7 mandate-line shape and 8c.8 plan schema must stay bit-identical to `/session-start` Step 3 and `/session-plan` Step 7, and the `logs/.prime-mtime` and `logs/.session-marker` contracts are read by `/session-start` Step 0.5, `/session-plan` Step 0 sub-step 0, and `/wrap-session` Step 3.5/1.5. The command is well-grounded in `principles.md § OP-3` (loud over silent) and `risk-topology.md § 1 High` (the `.prime-mtime` marker is named there explicitly).

## Innovations proposed

- **Cap the auto-mode plan-derivation inline blocks at one shared reference.** `risk-topology.md § R-7` — Step 8c.4 and 8c.5 re-derive mandate fields and plan fields by quoting `/session-start` Step 2 and `/session-plan` Step 1.5+2+5–7 verbatim. The duplication is a three-end contract that grows every time the originals are touched (per `principles.md § DR-7` — speculative coupling). The right answer is to factor 8c.4/8c.5 into a "see consumer contract: `/session-start` Step 2 § Field derivation" reference and a "see consumer contract: `/session-plan` Steps 1.5/2/5–7" reference, leaving only the auto-mode-specific deltas (no confirmation prompt, `(inferred)`/`(none stated)` defaults, mandate-without-prose-tail) inline. Affects: `.claude/commands/prime.md` Step 8c.4 and 8c.5. Risk class: minor (refactor, no contract drift if the references are exact).
- **Emit a single `auto:N` line in `.session-marker` payload to enable Phase 2 chain-aware foreign-write guard.** `principles.md § OP-9` (binding driver, not licensing speculation) — the deferred 2026-05-28 entry in `friction-log.md` § id-31 names the exact gap: the `PRIME_RAN=1` binary in `/wrap-session` Step 3.5 false-positives on chained `2,3 auto`. The proposed fix is gated on id-31 Phase 2 (marker-scoped session-notes headers), which is the right sequencing — but `/prime` Step 8c can already write the per-session task counter under the current marker (`{YYYY-MM-DD} S{N} auto:{K}` instead of `{YYYY-MM-DD} S{N}`) without breaking Phase 1's write-only contract. This pre-stages Phase 2's consumer-side fix without changing today's behavior. Affects: `.claude/commands/prime.md` Step 8a.3.a / 8b.3.a / 8c.3 marker write blocks. Risk class: minor (additive payload, no current consumer).
- **Surface the menu-zero state in step 6 with a hint at `/handoff` continuity mode.** `principles.md § OP-1` (compound AI value) — current behavior on zero menu items is `No tracked next steps — tell me what to work on.` which forces the operator to pick from cold context. `/handoff` continuity mode and Step 1b's scratchpad detector already cover the "last session left a resumable scratchpad" path, but when neither fires AND `next-up.md` is empty AND nothing urgent, the operator's frequent next move is to open `/open-items` (already linked in the brief footer) or describe work freely. Adding a one-line hint `(no scratchpad, no next-up, no urgent — pick from /open-items or describe the work)` makes the operator's next action explicit. Affects: `.claude/commands/prime.md` Step 6 zero-menu replacement line. Risk class: none (chat-only string change).

## Brokenness / drift noted

- **Currency drift — frontmatter shape lags unified-docs convention.** Evidence: `.claude/commands/prime.md` lines 1–3 declare only `model: sonnet`. Current `https://code.claude.com/docs/en/skills` documents `description`, `argument-hint`, `effort`, `allowed-tools`, `disable-model-invocation`, `user-invocable` as the canonical optional frontmatter set for the unified slash-command/skill shape. The companion command `/session-plan` already declares `argument-hint` and `effort` (lines 1–5 of `session-plan.md`); `/prime` lags. Severity: Minor (cosmetic — does not break invocation, but the unified docs treat the command as a skill-shaped resource and `argument-hint` would surface autocomplete behavior that the operator would benefit from).
- **Step 0 directional inconsistency — `CWD_REPO` is established before `AI_RESOURCES` is named.** Evidence: lines 11–14 of `prime.md`. The instruction reads "Determine the cwd's git root: `CWD_REPO=$(...)`. If this fails, note … and skip to step 1. Define `AI_RESOURCES=...`." The `AI_RESOURCES` definition lands after the skip-to-step-1 branch, which means the skip path silently drops `AI_RESOURCES` and Step 1a's "if `$CWD_REPO` differs from `$AI_RESOURCES`" comparison runs against an undefined variable. Severity: Substantive (logic bug on the not-a-git-repo path — Step 1a's dual-repo cross-check silently falls back to single-repo). The fix is one line: move the `AI_RESOURCES` definition above the cwd-root determination, or to the top of Step 0 unconditionally.
- **Currency check status: passed for `code.claude.com/docs/en/skills`** — pinned URL fetched successfully on 2026-05-29; comparison documented in the Minor finding above.
